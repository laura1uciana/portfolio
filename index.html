Laura Luciana is a freelance critic, performer, and arts worker thinking about creative labour and jerking off, now based in NYC.



She recently was awarded First Class Honours for her Art Theory (BArtTh(Hons) UNSW) thesis:

WE WANK EVERYTHING: A Nonsovereign Reappraisal of Liberatory Collective Masturbation in Sydney’s Jerk-Off Spaces


Recently, she performed Enduring Cruel Optimism: a Performance Reading with Levent Can Kaya at Schmick Contemporary, and read at Gus McGrath's Queens of the Circulating Library and served as editor for his forthcoming book. Her performance lecture Critical Playlisting was conducted at Art Gallery of New South Wales for the issue of arts journal Framework: React.


Laura's criticism has been commissioned by un Projects, Memo Review, and Art Monthly; she has written on digital art, taboo, and sex in visual culture, and won't tire of it soon.


In collaboration with film critic Charles Carrall, she has recorded and performed as Vanity Project for over four years. Vanity Project is primarily a podcast, but also often critical writing, live performance and reading, events and curatorial projects. They have emceed parties at the Art Gallery of NSW (AGNSW), performed at the Ace Hotel, been Artists In Residence at The Lock Up, and commissioned by Un Extended. Most recently they had their third birthday in residence at Brand X for the ninth season of the Flying Nun program.
