Enduring Cruel Optimism: A Performance Reading
 12.05.2025

On Monday 12th May we committed to an Endurance Study #1: a reading of Lauren Berlant’s iconic Cruel Optimism. We are honoured to read under Sydney Jarrett’s An Order Into Another, who is always thinking about the ways that being in public corrals us, our behaviours and desires, in sometimes cruel, sometimes optimistic ways. In the spirit of cruel optimism we endure labour, friendship, love, eating, mourning, citizenship. We attach to each other and to feelings in sustaining and subordinating ways. You, our audience, shared this burden by listening and reading with us.

Thank you to the scholars who inspire us, particularly to the spirit of study by Snack Syndicate, who in no small part introduced us to Berlant’s work, and support our commitment to studying together. Thank you to Schmick, in particular to Ashton and Clare who endured to the end. Thank you especially to Clare for your undying support and commitment to the project.

[Memo Review]
Garden Variety Dykes
 19.04.2025

Garden Variety Dykes isn’t about making encoded dyke sexualities visible, but instead reviving the political- and action-focussed era of 90s lesbian organising and making. Just like the 1994 text, Haggarty has assembled an ambitious array of ways to live in our gardens with our sexualities, cultivating their relationship to the earth in a time when this is imperilled by precarious rental contracts and the climate crisis. Gardening is sweaty, sexy work, the earth is erotic, maybe because of the ways that we touch it, maybe because its bounty is so seductive.











[framework] Refuse: Critical Playlisting performance lecture
 06.11.2024

Curated by Sydney Jarrett, and held at the Art Gallery of New South Wales. The Critical Playlisting performance lecture is an instructional seminar that encourages a community and site specific mode of listening to and comsuming music. By harnessing a nostalgia for the compilation CD, rejecting streaming, and buying music, we can understand our relationship to the creation and ownership of listening and music. It is imperative to divest from the streaming model in favour of other new or nostalgic models of music listening.

 26.08.2024

[Memo Review]
Jus Diddit: A Community Unfurling
 23.03.2024

Taking Western Sydney as its starting point, Jus Diddit asks: what does it take to create vibrant communities and resonant subcultures, and where do you start? For the local ravers, punks, and queers celebrated and archived in Jus Diddit, the answer is simple: they just did it. Curators Levent Can Kaya and Carmen Mercedes Gago Schieb speak in the language of the scenes because they inhabit these scenes.
